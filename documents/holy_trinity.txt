Our Mission

Rooted in Christ’s compassion and the example of St. Vincent de Paul, we commit ourselves to serving those in need.

Following our Lord Jesus Christ and his disciple St. Vincent de Paul, we at the Holy Trinity Center, as part of the parish community outreach of Holy Trinity Catholic Church, share a deep concern for the suffering of the poor.

We invite all people to unite in listening and responding to the cries of those in need. Such response would include assistance with most basic needs of life, using a variety of means and resources. The underlying principle in such assistance is that of helping the poor and their families regardless of religious affiliation or ethnicity.
Our Values

At our table, all are welcomed as family.

At the heart of our pantry is a spirit of dignity, hospitality, and compassion. Each person who comes through our doors is valued for who they are, not defined by their circumstances. By creating a space where relationships are built and hope is nurtured, we affirm the God-given worth of all who come through our doors. Generosity, kindness, and solidarity guide our work, reminding us that when we serve together, we strengthen not only individuals and families, but the whole community.
Appointment Process

    Pantry service is appointment-based
    First-time visitors: come as a walk-in during pantry hours to receive food and be scheduled for their first appointment
    All future appointments are scheduled in-house during pantry hours
    If you miss an appointment: email foodpantry@htccd.org to request a make-up appointment (responses within 2–3 days)
    30-minute grace period: late arrivals may have their slot given to another neighbor
    Limited walk-ins available but not guaranteed

Service Area

    Pantry serves neighbors in Central Dallas and surrounding communities 
    Reference familiar neighborhoods: Cedar Springs, Oak Lawn, Uptown, Turtle Creek, Love Field, and nearby areas 

Frequency of Service

    General neighbors: every two weeks
    Seniors: every two weeks (ages 60+)
